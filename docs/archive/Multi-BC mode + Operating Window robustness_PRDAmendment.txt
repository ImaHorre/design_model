 Plan: Multi-BC Modes + Operating Window Robustness — PRD Amendment

 Context

 The user wants to understand whether the model should support multiple boundary-condition
 (BC) configurations depending on the stage of design or experimentation, and whether the
 existing operating map machinery can be surfaced as a first-class "robustness" output
 alongside every design result. The goal is to formalize this as a future-feature addition
 to docs/PRD_v1.md.

 ---
 Restating the User's Intent

 Two separate but linked ideas:

 Idea 1 — Multiple BC Modes

 At different stages of device development the user knows different things:
 - Design stage: "I want 10% emulsion at 5 mL/hr total. What geometry do I need?"
 → natural inputs are Q_oil and Q_water (flow-flow).
 - Operation stage: "I have this device. Oil controller is at 200 mbar, pump at 5 mL/hr.
 What will it do?" → natural inputs are Po_in and Qw_in (pressure-flow, current Mode A).
 - Future: "What if both channels are pressure-driven?" → Po_in and Pw_in (pressure-pressure).

 Idea 2 — Operating Window Robustness Explorer

 Once a design exists, automatically answer: "how robust is this design?"
 - Show the Po and Qw ranges over which the device stays well-behaved (all rungs active,
 uniformity acceptable, no blowout).
 - Flag: is the design point in the middle of a wide window (robust) or on the edge of
 a narrow one (fragile)?
 - Implication: a wide window means the target emulsion concentration may be achievable at
 much higher throughput — the initial design point may be far from the device's ceiling.

 ---
 Pros and Cons of Multiple BC Modes

 Mode A: Po_in + Qw_in  (current — pressure-flow)

 Pros:
 - Matches the actual lab setup most commonly used (pressure controller on oil, syringe pump
 on water).
 - Po_in is a direct safety input — you can immediately check delamination risk.
 - Gives a physically grounded operating point that reflects what happens on the bench.

 Cons:
 - Emulsion concentration (oil fraction) is an OUTPUT not an INPUT — harder to design to a
 target ratio.
 - Can't specify "I want 10% oil concentration" directly; have to iterate on Po to find the
 point where Q_oil/Q_water ≈ 0.1.

 Mode B: Qo_in + Qw_in  (design mode — flow-flow, seed equivalent)

 Pros:
 - Emulsion ratio is directly set: emulsion_ratio = Qo_in / (Qo_in + Qw_in).
 - Total throughput is directly set: Q_total = Qo_in + Qw_in.
 - Natural for "design from target" workflow: given target ratio and throughput, the solver
 finds the pressures that would be needed.
 - Gives the designer a required oil inlet pressure as an OUTPUT — can immediately flag if
 it exceeds delamination limits.

 Cons:
 - Does NOT directly tell you what pressure controller to set in the lab.
 - The required pressure is an output and may be impractical (too high/too low) for the
 geometry chosen.
 - Solver is slightly more complex: oil inlet row must change from Dirichlet (fixed pressure)
 to Neumann (injected flow), and a separate pressure anchor must be chosen (water outlet = 0).
 - The requested Qo_in / Qw_in combination may be physically unachievable for a given geometry
 (system may have no stable solution at those flows).

 Mode C: Po_in + Po_water_out  (both pressure-controlled)

 Pros:
 - Both inlets/outlets are pressure references — appropriate for gravity-driven or
 pressurised reservoir setups.
 Cons:
 - Total flow is not directly set — it is determined entirely by geometry and pressure
 difference. Makes throughput target-setting indirect.
 - Less common in lab practice for step-emulsification.
 - Lower priority.

 Recommendation

 Implement Mode A (done) and Mode B (design mode) as the two primary BC modes.
 Mode C is lower priority and can be added later.

 ---
 Pros and Cons of the Operating Window Robustness Feature

 What it would do

 After evaluate_candidate() runs at the design point (Po*, Qw*), it automatically runs a
 small operating map sweep around that point and reports:
 - window_width_mbar at Qw*: the contiguous stable Po range at the design water flow.
 - margin_lower_mbar: distance from Po* down to P_min_ok.
 - margin_upper_mbar: distance from Po* up to P_max_ok.
 - window_center_mbar: optimal Po for maximum margin at Qw*.
 - max_Qw_stable_mlhr: the maximum water flow at which any stable Po window exists.
 - max_throughput_Q_total: Q_oil + Q_water at the widest stable window point.

 Pros:
 - Directly answers "is this design robust?" without the user having to manually set up
 a sweep.
 - Enables Pareto: designs with equal throughput but different window widths can be ranked
 — a key gap identified in the exploration (§6.3 tradeoff plots cannot currently be built
 because window_width is not in the sweep table).
 - Catches the case where the design point is far from the device ceiling: user can
 immediately see "this device can run at 10x throughput and stay stable — you may be
 under-utilising it".
 - Flags fragile designs early: "your stable Po window is only 8 mbar wide".

 Cons:
 - Adds compute time to every evaluate_candidate() call (each sweep point is an iterative
 solve). Mitigated by making it opt-in via a flag (compute_robustness=True).
 - Requires choosing sensible grid bounds and resolution automatically (could use ±50%
 around design point as a default heuristic).
 - Adds fields to the sweep DataFrame that some users may not need.

 ---
 What Already Exists (Do Not Re-Implement)

 From the exploration:
 - compute_operating_map(config, Po_grid, Qw_grid) — already computes windows_strict
 and windows_relaxed with window_width, window_center, P_min_ok, P_max_ok.
 Located in stepgen/design/operating_map.py.
 - OperatingWindow dataclass already has all required robustness fields.
 - blowout_dP_Pa is already wired into strict window criteria.
 - solve_linear(config, Q_oil, Q_water) in stepgen/models/hydraulics.py already
 implements Q-Q BCs but is NOT wired into the iterative threshold solve path.

 The robustness feature is primarily a matter of calling compute_operating_map from
 within evaluate_candidate and surfacing the window fields — not new physics.

 ---
 Proposed PRD Additions

 New Section 3.7 — Boundary Condition Modes

 ## 3.7 Boundary Condition Modes

 Mode A (default, operating mode):
   - Oil inlet: pressure-controlled (Po_in [mbar])
   - Water inlet: flow-controlled (Qw_in [mL/hr])
   - Use when: device exists, lab instruments are known.
   - Output: Q_oil_total, active_fraction, droplet metrics, operating window.

 Mode B (design mode):
   - Oil inlet: flow-controlled (Qo_in [mL/hr])
   - Water inlet: flow-controlled (Qw_in [mL/hr])
   - Emulsion ratio is directly set: = Qo_in / (Qo_in + Qw_in)
   - Use when: designing from throughput/concentration targets.
   - Output: required Po_in (from solver), delamination check, droplet metrics.

 Both modes share the same rung threshold model, regime classification,
 droplet size model, and operating window analysis.

 New Section 6.4 — Operating Window Robustness

 ## 6.4 Operating Window Robustness

 For any design candidate, at the nominated operating point (Po*, Qw*):

 1. Run a local operating map sweep over Po ∈ [Po* × α_lo, Po* × α_hi]
    and Qw ∈ [Qw* × β_lo, Qw* × β_hi].  Default multipliers: 0.2–3.0 for Po,
    0.5–5.0 for Qw.

 2. Extract the strict window at Qw*:
    - window_width_mbar
    - margin_lower_mbar  = Po* - P_min_ok
    - margin_upper_mbar  = P_max_ok - Po*
    - window_center_mbar
    - is_design_point_centered (bool: margin_lower within 20% of margin_upper)

 3. Find the maximum throughput stable operating point:
    - max_stable_Qw_mlhr: highest Qw at which any strict window is open.
    - max_stable_Q_total_mlhr: Q_oil + Q_water at the widest stable window.
    - throughput_headroom_pct: (max_stable_Q_total / Q_total_design - 1) × 100

 4. Robustness classification:
    - ROBUST: window_width > 100 mbar AND margin_lower > 20 mbar AND margin_upper > 20 mbar
    - MODERATE: window open, margin < 20 mbar on either side
    - TIGHT: window_width < 30 mbar
    - CLOSED: no stable window at Qw*

 These fields are appended to evaluate_candidate() output and the sweep DataFrame,
 enabling Pareto plots of throughput vs window_width (completing PRD §6.3).

 Updated Section 12 — New Acceptance Criteria

 AC10 — Mode B (flow-flow) produces pressures consistent with Mode A when
        Po_in is matched to the Mode B solver's output P_oil_inlet.
 AC11 — window_width_mbar is present in sweep DataFrame rows.
 AC12 — throughput_headroom_pct is positive and finite for all passing candidates.
 AC13 — Robustness classification matches manual inspection of operating map for
        at least 3 reference geometries.
